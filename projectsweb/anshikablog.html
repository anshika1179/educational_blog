From Top of the Class to Finding Balance: My Journey Through School and College

⌂ Home
When I look back on my school life, I can't help but feel nostalgic for the days when I excelled academically. I was that top student, always eager to answer questions, participate in discussions, and maintain a perfect score. Confidence was my constant companion, and I felt unstoppable.

However, transitioning to college was a different story. The competition was fiercer, the expectations higher, and suddenly, I found myself slipping from the top. My grades began to average out, and with each passing day, my confidence started to waver. I questioned my abilities and wondered if I could ever regain that sense of achievement I once had.

But instead of succumbing to self-doubt, I decided to take proactive steps to rebuild my skills and confidence. Here's what I've been doing to find my balance again, and these are strategies that every student can apply:

Embrace a Growth Mindset: Recognize that intelligence and abilities can be developed with effort and perseverance. Mistakes are opportunities to learn and grow.

Seek Help When Needed: Don't hesitate to ask professors, peers, or mentors for guidance. Collaboration and support from others can provide new insights and understanding.

Set Realistic Goals:Break down larger tasks into manageable steps. Celebrate small victories along the way to stay motivated.

Practice Self-Care: Balance is crucial. Ensure you get enough rest, eat healthily, exercise, and take time for hobbies and relaxation.

Stay Organized:Use planners, calendars, or apps to keep track of assignments, deadlines, and study schedules. Staying organized reduces stress and enhances productivity. Continuous Learning: Take advantage of online courses, workshops, and seminars to acquire new skills and knowledge beyond the classroom.

Reflect and Adjust:Regularly reflect on your progress, identify areas for improvement, and adjust your strategies accordingly.

Through these practices, I've slowly begun to regain my confidence and improve my academic performance. College may have its challenges, but with determination and the right approach, we can navigate through it and come out stronger.

- ANSHIKA
